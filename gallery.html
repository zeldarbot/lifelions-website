"The Pride"

LifeLions — Brand Anthem

02 — The line becomes a lion
04 — A caregiver, a doctor, a dog, a child — all joining
05 — Hands holding, meals shared, yoga at sunrise
06 — A garden, a painting, a puzzle, prayer in soft light
07 — The lion at center, surrounded by its pride
08 — "You are not alone. You are pride."
Narration Script — A Letter from Justin

Dear Mom,

I didn't know what to do. So I drew a line.

One line on empty paper. And the line became a lion. And the lion started walking. And behind it — I saw you.

You, reaching for my hand across a kitchen table. You, forgetting and then remembering and then forgetting again. You, still you inside all of it.

And behind you — everyone. The son buried in paperwork. The woman who said I don't need help and meant I'm terrified. The firefighter who couldn't find the exit. The daughter up at 4 AM.

The caregiver. The doctor. The dog who just showed up and stayed.

We are not a company. We are a pride. The way lions are — fierce, together, protecting what matters.

I drew a line because I didn't know what else to do.

The line became this.

You are not alone. You are pride.

— LifeLions

"The Numbers"

LionGuard — AI Health Concierge
01 — Maria watches her mother stare blankly out a window
02 — Holding the blood test results, overwhelmed
03 — Close-up: fear, the paper crumpled in her hands
04 — Hands holding a phone — clarity emerging, light breaking through
05 — Fear transforms to understanding, a subtle smile
06 — Hiking the Arizona desert at sunrise, strong and determined
Narration Script — Maria's Voice

I watched my mother disappear behind her own eyes.

Not all at once. In numbers. The ones the doctor circled in red. The ones I Googled at two in the morning, hands shaking, screen blurring.

I held that paper like a verdict. Like the world had already decided.

But numbers don't know my mother. They don't know the woman who crossed a border with nothing and built everything. They don't know her hands — how they braided my hair, how they held me when the world was too loud.

So I learned what the numbers actually meant. Not a sentence. A signal. Not too late — just in time.

I started walking. Desert mornings, before the heat. One foot, then the other. The same way she taught me.

The numbers are just numbers. What I do with them — that's the story.

You are not alone. You are pride.

"The Paperwork Monster"

LionShield — Insurance Navigator
01 — David at the kitchen table, midnight, papers everywhere
02 — Papers multiplying into a surreal monster
03 — Head in hands, defeated
04 — A golden shield dissolves the paper monster
05 — Morning — David and his father in the garden, peaceful
06 — Their hands together. He can't remember, but he feels the love.
Narration Script — David's Voice

It started with one envelope.

Then ten. Then a tower. Insurance codes I couldn't pronounce. Denials for things Dad needed yesterday. Forms that asked questions no human should have to answer at midnight.

I am not a lawyer. I am not a nurse. I am a son whose father forgot his name on a Tuesday, and the world responded with paperwork.

The monster grew. It fed on my sleep, my patience, the hours I should've spent just sitting with him.

Then someone said: You don't have to fight this alone.

And the monster — the one made of paper and bureaucracy and impossible choices — it shrank. Not because it disappeared. Because I wasn't facing it by myself anymore.

This morning, Dad and I sat in the garden. He doesn't know my name. But he held my hand. And that was everything.

You are not alone. You are pride.

"The Stranger in the House"

LionHeart Care — Caregiver Marketplace
01 — Sandra, arms crossed, defiant in her chair
02 — A cold, clinical caregiver in the doorway
03 — Sandra looking out the window, alone
04 — Rosa arrives with a small potted plant
05 — Hands in garden soil together, arms uncrossed
06 — Sandra laughing, genuinely happy
Narration Script — Sandra's Voice

I didn't ask for help. I don't need help. I've run this house for fifty-two years.

They sent someone. Clipboard. Rubber shoes. She rearranged my kitchen and called me "sweetie."

I sat in my chair with my arms crossed and waited for her to leave.

Then Rosa came.

Rosa came with a potted plant and dirt under her fingernails. She didn't rearrange anything. She asked where I kept the good scissors. She laughed at the wrong things and I couldn't help laughing too.

We planted tomatoes.

My hands remembered before I did. The soil. The roots. Something about growing a thing from nothing — it brought me back to myself.

Rosa isn't a stranger. She's the person who bothered to find out who I was before I got sick.

That's not care. That's pride.

You are not alone. You are pride.

"Bear"

LionHeart Therapy — Pet Therapy
01 — James, retired firefighter, sitting alone in an empty house
02 — A massive Great Pyrenees led through the door
03 — James turning away, resistant
04 — Bear at his feet, patient. James ignoring him.
05 — Bear's head on James's chest. James crying with relief.
06 — James burying his face in Bear's fur
07 — James and Bear walking together, side by side
Narration Script — James's Voice

Forty years, I ran into burning buildings. I carried people out. That was the job.

Now the building is my own mind, and I can't find the exit.

My daughter brought a dog. A ridiculous, enormous, white dog named Bear. I told her to take him back.

Bear didn't listen.

He lay at my feet. He didn't need me to be strong. He didn't need me to remember. He just... stayed.

One morning I woke up and his head was on my chest. Two hundred pounds of warm, breathing patience. And I cried.

Not because I was sad. Because for the first time in months, I didn't feel like I was disappearing.

Bear doesn't know I was a firefighter. He doesn't know I'm forgetting. He just knows I'm here. And he's not leaving.

Neither am I.

You are not alone. You are pride.

"The Last Painting"

LionHeart Therapy — Art Therapy
01 — Walls covered in her paintings, Eleanor sits vacant
02 — Charcoal in her hand, her daughter watching with worry
03 — Her hand moves — shaky but purposeful
04 — The drawing emerging — a lion taking shape
05 — Eleanor smiling — the real smile, back at last
06 — The completed lion drawing, rough and magnificent
Narration Script — Eleanor's Daughter

Mom painted every day of her life. Watercolors. Oils. The walls of our house are a museum of everything she saw and loved.

Then she stopped seeing.

Not her eyes — her eyes are fine. But the thing behind them, the thing that made her pick up a brush and turn light into color — it went quiet.

We put charcoal in her hand. No expectation. No hope, honestly. Just... something to hold.

Her hand moved. Shaky. Slow. But purposeful.

She drew a lion.

Rough and raw and nothing like her old work. But alive. Every mark intentional, every stroke a choice her mind was still making.

She smiled. The real one. The one I thought was gone.

The disease took so much. But it didn't take this. It didn't take the artist. She's still in there, drawing lions in the dark.

You are not alone. You are pride.

"4 AM"

Caregiver Wellness
01 — Keisha awake at 4 AM, barely visible in shadow
02 — Blue light on her face, searching
03 — Other caregivers awake at 4 AM too
04 — Crying — release, not defeat
05 — Dawn light creeping through the window
06 — Sunrise — standing at the window, still tired but not alone
Narration Script — Keisha's Voice

Four in the morning, and I'm awake again.

Not because of an alarm. Because the worry has its own clock. Did I give her the right dose? Is she breathing okay? When did I last eat?

I pick up my phone. Not to scroll. To search. How to not lose yourself while losing someone.

And there they are. Other faces. Other 4 AMs. A woman in Ohio with the same dark circles. A man in Texas who hasn't slept through the night in eight months. A daughter in Phoenix who just typed: Is anyone else awake?

Yes. I'm awake.

And for the first time, being awake at four in the morning doesn't feel like drowning. It feels like finding the others who are treading water too.

Dawn comes. It always does. I'm still tired. But I'm not alone.

You are not alone. You are pride.

The Products

Every product is a form of protection

LionGuard

AI Health Concierge — "Your health, decoded."

Upload lab results, get plain-English interpretation with functional ranges. Track biomarker trends. Personalized supplement and lifestyle recommendations. Alzheimer's biomarker focus.

LionShield

Insurance Navigator — "We fight the paperwork."

AI agent that decodes coverage, files claims, generates denial appeal letters, discovers hidden benefits, and navigates Medicare/Medicaid.

PrideMatch

Caregiver Marketplace — "Find your family's perfect caregiver."

Deep matching beyond availability — personality, specialization, language, faith. Video intros, background checks, burnout prevention.

LionHeart

Holistic Care Programs — "Heal the whole person."

ReCODE Protocol guidance, Mediterranean/MIND diet meal plans, yoga, pet therapy, art therapy, faith and mindfulness, community gatherings.

PrideStories

Charcoal Animation Studio — "Every family has a story."

Original charcoal-animated short films. Custom family portraits and legacy stories. The art you see in this gallery — brought to life as animation.

DenKeeper

Smart Home — "Technology that watches over your pride."

Activity pattern monitoring, medication reminders, fall detection, family dashboard. Helping elderly people age safely at home.
